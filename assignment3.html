Data Visualization Redesign Process

Step One: The Visualization

I found this dataset very interesting as I am a dog person and used to have a dog. This visualization presents many interesting facts regarding dogs in NYC and the overall design is fun and witty. Hence, the design and the context caught my attention. At the same time, I also noticed that there are certain things could be adjusted such as the chart type, color usage, title naming, axis labeling, etc.

Original Data Visualization

Step Two: The Critique

Design 1

Strengths:

  • The use of dog breed pictures to represent each breed was a standout feature. It immediately grabbed attention and made the graph more visually engaging.
  • The color palette complemented the design well, blending harmoniously with the images of the dog breeds.

Weaknesses:

  • The fonts and colors of the information at the bottom were unclear and needed improvement for better readability.

Design 2

Strengths:

  • The bold title clearly conveyed the graph's purpose and was the first element to catch the eye.
  • The font was easy to read, and the use of dog breed images made the visualization visually appealing.

Weaknesses:

  • The design felt a bit cluttered due to the large number of breeds (20 in total), which spread the scale too broadly.
  • Some elements, like the blue dots, lacked annotations, making them confusing to interpret.

Design 3

Strengths:

  • The bright green background and central Labrador breed in motion were visually prominent, drawing attention immediately.
  • Annotations were useful in providing context, such as the data source.

Weaknesses:

  • The sprint line on the right side didn’t relate meaningfully to the breeds and could mislead the audience.
  • The background image was somewhat distracting, making the data harder to focus on. A bar chart might better represent the data.

Design 4

Strengths:

  • The bone-shaped name tags were a cute, unique, and eye-catching design element.
  • The size of the tags was well-ordered, making it easy to follow the data.

Weaknesses:

  • The font color for the number of dogs beneath the tags needed improvement. Using black to contrast with the brown background would make it more readable.

Overall Strengths

  • Visual appeal: The use of images and unique shapes (like the bone tags) made the visualizations engaging and fun, which works well for the intended audience.
  • Clear focus: The titles and focal points, like dog breed images, helped guide the viewer's attention to the important aspects of each visualization.

Overall Weaknesses

  • Clutter: In some designs, like Design 2, having too many elements created a cluttered appearance that made it harder to interpret the data.
  • Color and accessibility issues: The use of red and green could cause issues for colorblind users. A more neutral or accessible color palette would be beneficial.
  • Annotations and labels: Some graphs lacked proper annotations (e.g., Design 2’s blue dots), making it hard to understand all the elements. Legends or clearer labeling would improve comprehension.

As the author has 6 designs, I chose 4 of them to redesign based on my critique above.

Synthesis

Suggested Improvements: Change the Color Scheme: Address the accessibility issue with the green and red colors, considering colorblind-friendly palettes. Switching to more neutral or contrasting colors (e.g., blue and darker shades) was a common recommendation. Icons and Symbols: Use symbols, like paw prints for dog distribution and bite marks for dog bites, to make the representation more intuitive. Refining the Message: Since the correlation between the two variables was not immediately clear, adding additional context or improving how the data relationship is visualized (e.g., through tooltips or labeling) could help. Influence on the Final Redesign: Based on the feedback, the redesign will focus on: Improving Accessibility: Redesigning the color palette to be more accessible for users with color blindness and avoiding traditional color associations (green for correct, red for wrong). Using Symbols: Incorporating visual symbols like icons to distinguish between different data points more clearly, ensuring the audience quickly understands what each plot represents. Enhancing Clarity: Adding visual aids or annotations that more explicitly show the relationship between dog distribution and dog bite cases to improve the graph's interpretability.
