Wildfire Data Visualization Project Part II

Wireframes / Storyboards

User Research

    Target Audience

    My audience for this project could be categorized in 3 groups: Avid Hiking Enthusiasts, California Residents, and Environmentally Conscious Individuals.

  1. Avid Hiking Enthusiasts

    Hiking enthusiasts are deeply connected to California’s stunning landscapes, from the towering redwoods to the expansive national parks. The growing wildfire crisis poses a direct threat to the trails and natural beauty they cherish. By highlighting the risks that wildfires pose to their favorite hiking spots, this project can educate hikers on the importance of wildfire prevention and awareness. Encouraging them to stay informed about local fire risks and support conservation efforts aligns with their love for the outdoors.

  2. California Residents

    As the most wildfire-prone state in the U.S., California residents are living on the frontlines of the wildfire crisis. Whether they are directly impacted by evacuations or dealing with smoke-filled skies, this audience knows firsthand the devastation wildfires bring. The project aims to inform residents about the causes, economic costs, and environmental damage of wildfires in their state, motivating them to take preventative actions, advocate for stronger climate policies, and protect their communities.

  3. Environmentally Conscious Individuals

    People who care about climate change and environmental sustainability are key allies in the fight against wildfires. For this group, the connection between rising wildfire occurrences and broader environmental issues like global warming is particularly compelling. Through data visualizations that show the impact of wildfires on forests, air quality, and wildlife, this project appeals to their commitment to preserving the planet. They are likely to support long-term solutions, such as reforestation and climate legislation, that mitigate wildfire risks.

Identifying Interview Representatives

For this project, I chose three interviewees who represent the key demographics I am targeting: a California resident, a hiking enthusiast, and one environmental advocate. These individuals reflect the diversity of people who are most affected by wildfires and who care deeply about the issue.

    Interview Script

  1. Clarity of Visuals

    Question: What message do you think these visualizations are trying to convey? Do you find them clear and impactful? Is there anything you would improve?

  2. Effectiveness of Call to Action

    Question: As someone who loves the environment or enjoys outdoor activities, would these visuals motivate you to take action, such as advocating for better fire prevention measures or staying more informed about fire risks?

  3. Engagement with the Story

    Question: Are you engaged with the narrative being told about the wildfire crisis? Do you feel the story is structured well, from setup to conflict and resolution?

  4. Improvement

    Question: Is there anything the presentation could have done more effectively to make the call to actions better/is there something that is done well and I should emphasize more?

Identified Changes for Part III

    Conflict and Story is Clear, but Could Use More Personal Stories

  • Pattern: While the interviewees understood the main conflict (wildfire risks), they felt that adding personal stories would strengthen the emotional impact.
  • Change: I will incorporate personal anecdotes from individuals directly affected by wildfires. This will make the conflict more relatable and compelling, helping to emotionally engage the audience and emphasize the urgency of wildfire prevention.

    Call to Actions Need More Specificity and Practicality

  • Pattern: The interviewees felt the call to action was important but could benefit from clearer, more actionable steps, especially in terms of how individuals can get involved.
  • Change: I will simplify the calls to action, making them more specific to the audience. For hikers, I’ll include steps like "Join a fire prevention group" or "Follow fire safety guidelines on trails." For environmentally conscious individuals, I’ll provide links to advocacy organizations and petitions to encourage immediate action.

    More Practical Steps and Resources for Audience Engagement

  • Pattern: The interviewees appreciated the suggestions but felt there could be more practical steps, particularly for hikers and policymakers, to get involved.
  • Change: I will add more detailed resources for audience members, such as links to fire prevention organizations, local petitions, and specific legislation. This will make the call to action more actionable and give the audience a clear path toward engagement.
